Political Campaign Contributions

On January 21, 2010, the United States Supreme Court ruled that the government cannot restrict the amount a for-profit corporation corporation, labor union or voluntary association donates to a Political Action Committee (PAC), as doing so would restrict free speech. Even though the court held that any individual or organization could not make direct contributions to a candidate running for office, many were concerned with whether donations to a candidate's political action committee would affect a candidates policy decisions when in office. PACs provide most of the logistical support for candidates running for office (running advertisements, organizing trips, planning speeches, etc.), so if a politician votes against the interest of an organization providing donations, that candidate would risk losing those PAC donations in the next election cycle.

Our website examines if campaign finances influence how a politican makes decisions by examining the top contributors to a politican's campaign and their voting records.
